INTRODUCTION

Importance of Internship

Internship is an essential part of being a student in Information Technology because it marks the shift from academic learning to real-world experience. While school teaches us the theories and concepts, internship allows us to apply them in actual situations. It introduces us to real challenges that cannot be fully learned through books alone.

This experience does not only improve my technical skills but also helps shape my character. I learn how to collaborate with others, manage my time effectively, communicate professionally, and handle pressure in the workplace. It also helps me discover which area of IT I am most interested in, whether it is development, networking, or system support.

Overall, internships contribute to both my personal and professional growth. They give me a clearer understanding of what working in the industry is like and prepare me to face future responsibilities with greater confidence and direction after graduation.

Objectives of Internship

The internship aims to provide students with hands-on experience and enhance their competencies in the field of Information Technology, particularly in the Billing Office of Occidental Mindoro Electric Cooperative (OMECO).

1. Ensure that students acquire knowledge, technical skills, and competencies related to billing operations such as data encoding and record management.

2. Improve problem-solving and analytical skills in handling billing concerns, account verification, and charge computation.

3. Enhance office skills in processing billing records, generating reports, and maintaining organized files efficiently.

4. Engage students in real office tasks and expose them to professional workplace practices and customer service interactions.

5. Encourage discipline, teamwork, punctuality, and adherence to company rules and procedures.

Time and Place of Internship

Time of practicum: Monday–Friday (8:00 AM – 12:00 NN and 1:00 PM – 5:00 PM)

The on-the-job training was conducted from February 2, 2026 to April 30, 2026 at Occidental Mindoro Electric Cooperative (OMECO), located along M. H. del Pilar Street, Barangay VI, San Jose, Occidental Mindoro. I was assigned to the Billing Office, where I completed a total of 486 hours of office work.
